#Car-Sales-Analysis-Bright-Motors
 Project Overview
This project was completed as part of the BrightLearn Data Analytics Case Study, where I assumed the role of a Junior Data Analyst at Bright Motors — a dealership that has recently appointed a new Head of Sales.
The goal was to analyse historical car sales data and deliver actionable insights to support strategic decisions around revenue growth, inventory optimisation, and dealership expansion.

#Objectives

Identify which car makes and models generate the most revenue
Explore the relationship between price, mileage, and year of manufacture
Determine which regions/locations have the highest sales volumes
Uncover emerging trends in customer purchasing preferences
Provide recommendations to improve dealership profitability and efficiency

#Analysis Tasks
Task 1 — Planning & Architecture

Created a data flow diagram outlining the full pipeline from raw data to presentation layer
Mapped key insights to deliver and defined core KPIs

Task 2 — Data Processing SQL on databricks 

Converted Excel file to CSV and loaded into databricks
Cleaned and transformed data:

Converted text-based prices to numeric format
Created total_revenue column
Calculated profit_margin and categorised cars into High / Medium / Low Margin tiers
Grouped transactions by month, quarter, and year


Task 3 — Visualisation (Datastudio)

Built interactive dashboards with slicers for Region, Fuel Type, and Year
Included trend charts, revenue breakdowns, and regional heatmaps
Added short insight annotations on key visuals

Task 4 — Presentation

Delivered a concise, visually appealing presentation summarising all findings and strategic recommendations for the new Head of Sales.

Recommendations

Expand dealership presence in Gauteng and Western Cape to capitalise on high-volume markets.
Stock more high-margin SUVs and sedans from top-performing makes to maximise revenue per unit.
Introduce hybrid/EV inventory to align with evolving customer preferences.
Implement mileage-based pricing tiers to standardise and optimise profit margins across the fleet.
Review slow-moving inventory in low-margin categories and consider promotional pricing strategies.
